Crack Candy

Note: Before you begin, wash your hands thoroughly with warm water and soap for at least 20 seconds. Clean your workspace and gather all ingredients and materialss.

Adult supervision is recommended for younger and inexperienced cooks.

Materials Needed:

  • 1 large jelly-roll pan or 2 smaller baking sheets
  • Aluminum foil
  • Nonstick cooking spray
  • Medium saucepan
  • Wooden spoon or heat-safe spatula
  • Butter knife or offset spatula
  • Freezer space (optional for cooling)
  • Airtight container for storage

Ingredients:

  • 35–40 saltine crackers (enough to cover your pan in a single layer)
  • 1 cup butter (2 sticks)
  • 1 cup light or dark brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate chips (or 1 1/3 cups)
  • Optional: Chopped nuts or candy sprinkles for topping

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Line your baking pan(s) with aluminum foil, making sure it covers the bottom and sides. Spray the foil with nonstick cooking spray.
  3. Arrange the saltine crackers salt-side down in a single layer across the pan. Try to fill the entire surface without overlapping.
  4. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter and brown sugar together over medium heat. Stir constantly until the mixture begins to boil.
  5. Continue boiling for 2–3 minutes, stirring gently, until the mixture turns a rich caramel color. Watch closely to avoid burning.
  6. Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  7. Carefully pour the hot caramel mixture over the crackers, spreading it evenly with a spoon or spatula to coat all the crackers.
  8. Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for about 5 minutes, or until the caramel is bubbling. Keep an eye on it—don’t let it burn.
  9. Remove the pan from the oven and immediately sprinkle the chocolate chips evenly over the hot caramel layer.
  10. Wait 1–2 minutes for the chocolate chips to soften, then use a butter knife or spatula to spread the melted chocolate evenly across the surface.
  11. Optional: Sprinkle chopped nuts or candy sprinkles on top while the chocolate is still soft.
  12. Let the pan cool completely at room temperature. For faster cooling, place it in the freezer for 15–20 minutes until fully set.
  13. Once hardened, peel off the foil and break the candy into bite-sized pieces.
  14. Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to one week (if it lasts that long!).

Tip: For a festive twist, use colored sprinkles or drizzle white chocolate over the top once cooled.
