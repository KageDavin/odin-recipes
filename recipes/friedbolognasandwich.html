Fried Bologna Sandwich

Note: Before you begin, wash your hands thoroughly with warm water and soap for at least 20 seconds. Clean your workspace and gather all ingredients and materialss.

Adult supervision is recommended for younger and inexperienced cooks.

Materials Needed:

  • Small frying pan
  • Butter knife or table knife
  • Spatula (optional but helpful)
  • Plate for assembling sandwich

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of white bread (or preferred sandwich bread)
  • 1–2 slices of bologna (thicker slices may need more cooking time)
  • 1/8 teaspoon cooking oil (vegetable, canola, or olive oil)
  • 1 slice of American cheese (white or yellow)
  • 1 tablespoon mustard (yellow or Dijon)

Directions:

  1. Place a small frying pan on a stove burner. Make sure the burner is off before placing the pan.
  2. Turn the burner on to medium heat. Allow the pan to warm up for about 1 minute.
  3. Add 1/8 teaspoon of oil to the pan and gently swirl to coat the bottom.
  4. Using a butter knife, make a small cut from the center of the bologna slice to the edge. This helps prevent it from curling while cooking.
  5. Carefully place the bologna slice(s) into the heated pan. Use a spatula if needed to avoid touching the hot pan.
  6. Cook the bologna for about 2–3 minutes on the first side, until it starts to brown slightly.
  7. Flip the bologna using a spatula and cook the other side for another 2–3 minutes.
  8. While the second side is browning, place the slice of cheese on top of the bologna to melt slightly.
  9. While the bologna finishes cooking, lay out the two slices of bread on a plate. Use the butter knife to spread mustard on one side of each slice.
  10. Once the bologna is browned and the cheese is slightly melted, turn off the burner and carefully remove the bologna from the pan.
  11. Place the hot bologna and cheese onto one slice of mustard-covered bread.
  12. Top with the second slice of bread, mustard side down, to complete the sandwich.
  13. Let the sandwich cool for 1–2 minutes before serving. Enjoy!

Optional: You can toast the bread in the pan for extra crunch, or add toppings like pickles, lettuce, or tomato for a twist.
