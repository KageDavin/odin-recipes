Traditional Bread Pudding

Note: Before you begin, wash your hands thoroughly with warm water and soap for at least 20 seconds. Clean your workspace and gather all ingredients and materialss.

Adult supervision is recommended for younger and inexperienced cooks.

Materials Needed:

  • 1.5-quart casserole dish (or similar oven-safe baking dish)
  • Large mixing bowl
  • Whisk or fork
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Oven mitts

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups bread, cubed (day-old buns, sandwich bread, or rolls work well)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups milk (whole milk preferred for richness)
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Lightly grease the casserole dish with butter or non-stick spray.
  3. Cut or tear bread into bite-sized cubes and place evenly into the casserole dish.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, melted butter, sugar, and cinnamon until well combined.
  5. Slowly pour the mixture over the bread cubes, making sure all pieces are soaked. Press down gently with a spoon or spatula to help the bread absorb the liquid.
  6. Let the mixture sit for 5–10 minutes to fully absorb (optional but recommended for better texture).
  7. Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake for 40–45 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and a knife inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Carefully remove from the oven using oven mitts and allow to cool for 10–15 minutes before serving.
  9. Enjoy warm, optionally topped with whipped cream, vanilla sauce, or a sprinkle of powdered sugar!

Tip: You can add raisins, chopped apples, or a splash of vanilla extract to the mixture for extra flavor.
